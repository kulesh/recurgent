> # Jaffna Kool

**Cuisine:** Sri Lankan (Jaffna Tamil)
**Description:** A traditional cooling beverage-porridge made from palmyra root flour, served chilled with various accompaniments. Popular street food in Jaffna, especially during hot weather.

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** 20 minutes | **Chill Time:** 2-3 hours
**Servings:** Serves 6-8 people

## Ingredients

### For Kool Base:
- 1 cup palmyra root flour (odiyal maavu)
- 8-10 cups water
- Salt to taste

### Accompaniments:
- Shaved ice
- Jaggery or palm sugar syrup
- Coconut milk
- Chopped onions
- Chopped green chilies
- Lime wedges
- Crushed dried prawns (optional)

## Instructions

1. Mix the palmyra root flour with 2 cups of cold water until smooth, ensuring no lumps.
2. Boil the remaining 6-8 cups of water in a large pot.
3. Slowly pour the flour mixture into the boiling water while stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
4. Add salt to taste and continue stirring.
5. Cook on medium heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring frequently until the mixture thickens to a porridge-like consistency.
6. Remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
7. Refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours until well chilled.
8. To serve: Place a generous portion of chilled kool in a bowl.
9. Add shaved ice on top.
10. Add your choice of accompaniments: jaggery syrup for sweetness, coconut milk for richness, chopped onions and green chilies for a savory kick.
11. Squeeze lime juice over the top.
12. Mix everything together and enjoy immediately.

## Tips

- Palmyra root flour can be found in Sri Lankan grocery stores or online specialty shops.
- The kool base can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
- Traditional street vendors offer multiple topping options - you can customize to your taste (sweet, savory, or mixed).
- Some variations include adding chopped green mango or other fruits.
- The consistency should be slightly thick but pourable - adjust water if needed.
